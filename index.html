So we must ask: who is responsible for collecting it, and why hasn't it been collected?
~21,000
Every year, a crowd the size of everyone gathered at the Red Fort on Independence Day — simply stops breathing.

This is not a statistic. These are mothers, fathers, children, and neighbours — people who breathed the same air as you, in the same city, year after year. The smog that chokes Delhi every winter does not show up on a death certificate as "air pollution." It hides behind the words "respiratory failure," "cardiac arrest," "cerebrovascular disease." And so the data that would hold anyone accountable — never gets recorded.

→ No official Indian government dataset directly links Delhi's AQI to cause-specific mortality.
→ No annual public health audit maps pollution exposure to hospital admissions or deaths.
→ The data used here is from IHME (Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation) — a global research body, not Indian public records.
→ The question is not whether people are dying. The question is: why isn't anyone counting them?
