Why the delay?

Your lungs remember
what AQI forgets.

AQI resets every midnight. Your lungs don't. This is what years of Delhi's air actually does slowly, silently, irreversibly

01
The particle enters
PM2.5 is smaller than your body's defenses were built for. It bypasses your nose, your throat, your airways and lands deep inside your lung tissue. From there, it enters your blood. You feel nothing. That is the point.
02
Years of quiet damage
Your immune system registers every particle as an attack. Every day, it responds. Month after month, year after year, it fights inflammation it cannot win in lung tissue, in artery walls, in the brain. No fever. No warning. Just a slow, invisible accumulation of damage that shows up only when it's too late.
03
The system gives out
Eventually, the body fails a heart attack, a stroke, a lung that finally collapses. The data shows this happens 12 to 18 months after peak pollution exposure. By then, the air that caused it has long since cleared. The death certificate will say heart disease. It will not mention Delhi's air.
Scale — 2024

What does 21,000 deaths actually look like?

Picture Republic Day at Red Fort 21,000 people gathered, shoulder to shoulder. Now imagine every single one of them dead by year's end. That is not a thought experiment. That is Delhi's 2024 death toll from pollution-linked illness. One crowd. One year. No official record linking any of it to the air

Red Fort, Delhi capacity of approx. 21,000 people on Republic Day

The data above points to a clear co-relationship between Delhi's air and these deaths
but official causal data connecting them does not publicly exist.

Correlation is not causation. But when the air worsens and deaths follow year after year, disease after disease at what point does correlation become impossible to ignore? India records AQI daily. It does not officially track how many people die because of it. There is no national registry that links a death certificate to years of pollution exposure. So we must ask: who is responsible for collecting this data, and why hasn't it been collected?
